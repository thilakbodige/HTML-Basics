There are 5 Seasons

Winter

The coldest season with the shortest daylight hours. Many regions experience snow, ice, and dormant plant life.

Summer

Given the time of day, the heat of the day is likely starting to subside slightly, but the warm evening air would still be quite noticeable.

Rainy

The impact of the rainy season on the environment is immediate and dramatic. The dry, cracked earth drinks deeply, transforming from a dusty brown to a rich, fertile hue.

Spring

A transitional season between winter and summer, characterized by warming temperatures, increasing daylight hours, and the revival of plant life

Autumn

Another transitional season, between summer and winter, marked by cooling temperatures, decreasing daylight hours, and the shedding of leaves by deciduous trees.
